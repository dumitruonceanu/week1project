Glad you’re here!

I’m Dumitru, a front-end web developer from Toronto. Let me tell you a short story of my recent journey.

With a background of Computer Science and I spent a couple years working on a Human-Computer Interaction project designing and building a new interface device, using a 3D printer to design and prototype, while writing the code that brought it to life. Following that, I joined a small startup company in the online auction space where I helped launch and manage the Toronto region, doing sales, customer service, and project management. After three years, I wanted to get back into development and decided to take the freelance route. I picked up some skills using online courses and tutorials while building a clients site. Now I’m spending the summer working through the HackerYou bootcamp program polishing my skills with the latest techniques, while building clean and functional websites.

Web development...and more!

When not writing code, I like to stay engaged with three communities I'm passionate about.

I have a deep interest in cities and local urban development. I spent 2 years writing freelance for UrbanToronto, while contributing as a community photographer.

I have a passion for discovering and spreading great ideas through the TED and TEDx communities. I've been involved with TEDxToronto as a volunteer and organizer for 4 years, and have been to numerous regional conferences including TEDActive and TEDxMidAtlantic.

As a hobyist, I enjoy tinkering with 3D designs and using my 3D printer to make some really cool objects. One of my favourite sites for open source objects is Thingiverse. You can also find my 3D printer listed on 3D Hubs.
